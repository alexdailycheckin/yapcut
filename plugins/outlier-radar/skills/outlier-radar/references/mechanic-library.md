# Mechanic library

Proven viral mechanics harvested from real outliers. Each is a reusable SHAPE:
pour different substance in. Grow this list every run. A mechanic earns a spot
only when a documented outlier used it.

## General / primary-lane video mechanics

| Mechanic | What it does | Source outlier (ref) |
|---|---|---|
| Steelman-then-demolish | State the popular myth as if you endorse it, then dismantle it with evidence. The fake-out hook does the algorithmic work. | Dr Simon Clark "Renewables are a scam!" |
| Counterintuitive status-flip | "The thing everyone measures is the wrong thing." Surprising + flattering to "get". | Taylor Conroy, how the rich think about money (~3M) |
| Free-value utility + urgency | A concrete thing the system already owes the viewer + the exact step to claim it. Sharing feels like a favour. | Pearlman "go get your money" (~5M) |
| Relatable-callout list | "Why [job/role] is so [under-described feeling]" + 4-5 hyper-specific insider proofs. Drives tagging. | Gabe Dannenbring, teaching overstimulating (~40M) |
| Before/after completion | Lead with the most extreme "before" in frame one, one unbroken satisfying reveal. Brain must finish the loop. | AJ Joyner alleyway pressure-wash (~30M) |
| Employee-POV / hidden-in-plain-sight | A real insider reveals what your "boring" thing can actually do. Beats corporate polish. | Staples Baddie |
| Engineered-tension pile-on | One credible person opens a tension; everyone with a matching scar piles on. Thread distributes itself. | Greg Isenberg VC-asleep thread |
| Coin-a-term | Name the tension with a sticky, portable label; people spread the word and it carries the idea. | Kyla Scanlon "token-maxxing" |
| Unignorable-number / fraud-or-genius | An audacious specific claim forces public argument = free reach. | MEDVi "$1.8B solo founder" debate |
| Lo-fi high-concept | A big idea on a hand-drawn napkin/diagram; crayon production signals authenticity, idea is debate bait. | Jazz Nuku consciousness Venn (~4M from tiny account) |
| Contrarian-relief bet | Bet publicly against the prevailing "you must do X" advice; relief + media-model flip. | Packy McCormick essay/curation bet |
| Satisfying-mechanism cold open | Lead with the single most satisfying visual moment the product makes, zero intro. | Cruise Cup / Beerzooka (~50M, $300k/mo) |
| Character-ritual serialisation | A weirdly specific persona turns a format into a returnable bit. | Logan Moffitt "the cucumber guy" |
| Stranger-transaction serialisation | A numbered "Part N" real-world experiment with a risky/random stranger as the variable; the open loop is who you'll meet. | @itsgabrielrivera "trying food off Marketplace" (~26-28M) |
| Self-snitch single-frame | One still photo + deadpan "bad news for my [X]" confession of a habit you're secretly proud of. No edit. Comments one-up. | @arinabult "bad news for my wallet" (~752k likes) |
| 1-vs-many arena | Manufactured asymmetric conflict: one person against twenty opposites. The visual is the hook before a word is spoken. | Jubilee "Surrounded" (~38M/episode) |
| Refuse-the-label-on-camera | Publicly reject the box someone puts you in; the correction itself is the shareable moment and the repositioning. | Kat Abughazaleh "I'm a researcher, not an influencer" |
| Unrepentant confession | Admit a manipulation/uncomfortable truth with zero remorse; the missing apology is what makes it travel (opposite of the contrition arc). | London "hate brings views" fake-news TikToker |
| Euphemism reveal | Use a socially-sanctioned excuse as the setup, hard-cut to the unglamorous truth it was covering. | @seetorra "sorry, I have therapy" |
| Reflex-bait redirect | Trigger an automatic emotional reflex (the "aww"), hold, then swap the payload at the reveal. | IG "poor little baby" product reveals |
| Meta A/B challenge | Address the algorithm as a character; the video is a referendum on itself, the comment-vote is the engine. | @itslifewithp "let's see what the algorithm prefers" |
| Glitch-locked freeze / trend-audio hijack | Let a flaw or beat baked into a trending sound dictate the exact freeze frame; the audio does the editing and lends its momentum. | Charli XCX "Rock Music" stuck-frame |
| One-line emotional range reel | A single sentence performed across 4+ emotional registers; an acting flex that begs to be duetted. | "four emotions" TikTok trend |
| Fake-institution host universe | Build a recurring fictional org and stay in character across many episodes; the format is the franchise, real participants supply material. | Druski "Coulda Been" universe |
| Third-party-verification-as-content | Take an existing public debate ("does this hack work?") and become the adjudicator: test others' viral claims and stamp a verdict. You inherit the debate's attention. | Vaseline "#VaselineVerified" (136M views, 43% sales uplift) |
| Show-the-receipts conflict | When someone powerful tries to suppress you, post the artifact (the legal letter, the angry email, the screenshot). The suppression attempt IS the hook; the receipt is the proof. | Adam Robinson / RB2B cease-and-desist post (~1,600 leads) |
| Deliberate-flaw earned media | Plant an obvious "mistake" people can't resist correcting (a misspelling, a wrong fact); the pile-on of corrections is free distribution. | Coors Light "Case of the Mondays" intentional misspelling (12.6B impressions) |
| Amplify-don't-reshoot | A creator already made the thing go viral. The distribution move is to reshare the original as-is and put your reach behind it, not remake it in a studio. | Dr Pepper resharing the creator jingle (42M views, then aired at the National Championship) |
| Literalise-a-phrase visual gag | Take a familiar idiom and stage it dead-literally; the gap between the figurative phrase and the absurd literal staging is the whole joke. Instantly clear on mute. | "A little birdie told me" hoodie trend (May 2026) |
| Taboo day-in-the-life VO | Narrate a true but judgment-bait / contradictory life-state in flat, hyper-specific deadpan VO over real b-roll; chain ~30 four-second micro-loops, each a tiny self-contained cliffhanger; the lifestyle itself starts the comment war. The hook is the identity CONTRADICTION, never the job title. | @solidheaston "unemployed living in my car" (284k likes, 9.6k saves, 2.5k shares) |

## Secondary / "Viral videos" lane mechanics

The relatable-reach lane. Borrow one of these and pour in your own niche's real world (its rituals, its recognisable characters, its unwritten rules, the moments only insiders know). The examples below are drawn from workplace-comedy outliers because that genre documents these shapes cleanly, but the mechanic is what transfers, not the office setting: swap in whatever world your audience lives in. Sources flagged verified / reported / estimated.

| Mechanic | What it does | Source outlier (ref) |
|---|---|---|
| Jargon-character monologue | Cold open straight into a hyper-specific persona; the accent + vocabulary density IS the joke; one tribe instantly recognises the voice and tags their people. | Corporate Erin / Lisa Beasley (reported 9-12M) |
| Deadpan literal misread | Take a soft in-group phrase 100% literally; the gap between what the jargon says and what it means is the payoff. | Corporate Brian "WFH optional" (640k+ likes) |
| POV archetype title-card | Name a universal archetype in the title card ("POV: your millennial manager"), then play it straight so the viewer casts a real person from their life. | Business Casualty POV series (~1.2M/entry) |
| Named-behaviour coinage | Coin or ride a sticky label for an unspoken habit ("task masking", "pipeline theater"); the name is quotable, searchable, and spreads itself. | Corporate Natalie "task masking" (Forbes ~1.1M cluster) |
| Insider truth reveal | "Here's how it ACTUALLY works": the official script vs the unwritten reality; shared as proof you're in on the secret. | Daily Dot new-hire reveal (reported ~7.6M) |
| Credentialed contrarian flip | State the consensus accusation, flip it, defend the blamed side using insider credibility ("Gen Z isn't lazy..."); drives saves + debate. | Emily the Recruiter (3M+, Fortune-profiled) |
| Out-of-touch authority satire | Become the tone-deaf authority figure so the audience laughs at a frustration they can't voice; recognition, not information. | Corporate Natalie RTO/exec skits |
| Format hijack / tonal whiplash | Pour high-stakes content into a beloved low-stakes template (GRWM, recipe, vlog); the mismatch is the hook. | GRWM-layoff trend (HRMorning-documented) |
| "Watch me realize" pre-frame | Self-aware pre-frame of a painful moment so the hook promises a REACTION to watch, not info; the face does the work. | Heather / itsgivingheather (est. ~6.8M) |
| Film-the-real-thing | Documentary footage of a feared real moment where the subject calmly refuses the script; reality beats acting. | Brittany Pietsch live layoff (verified 1.4-3.4M) |
| Things-that-should-be-illegal listicle | Numbered cold open promising a fast agreeable pile-on; each item a tiny payoff; comments fill with viewers' own. | Office-rant genre |
| Recurring character / saga | A serialised, instantly recognisable archetype people follow across episodes; misdirection drives each individual hit. | "Chit" / Jay Renshaw (verified ~25M) |
