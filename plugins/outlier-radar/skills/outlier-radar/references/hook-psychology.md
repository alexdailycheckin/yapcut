# The six psychological hook styles (apply to EVERY hook)

Distilled from a teardown of thousands of viral videos: six hook styles are
consistently outperforming everything else because each fires a specific
psychological response, and all six carry the two ingredients every hook needs,
**curiosity + contrast**. The best creators STACK two or more at once.

Use this file alongside `hook-library.md` (the wording pattern families) and
`virality-psychology.md` (the deeper principle lens). The families are the
WORDING patterns; these six are the WHY, the trigger underneath. Every
`text_hook` and `spoken_hook` should be classifiable as at least one of the
six. If it fires none of them, it is a topic, not a hook, rewrite it.

## 1. Crystal Ball (future access)
Make the viewer feel they are getting future information before everyone else.
- Psychology: humans are anxious about the future; prediction content gives
  comfort, control and relief.
- Patterns: "This is the future of [niche]." / "We're witnessing the death of
  [common thing]." / "In six months nobody will be doing [current practice]
  anymore."
- Maps to families: curiosity gap, warning/stakes.

## 2. Insider (I know something you don't)
Open by signalling exclusive knowledge; people hate feeling excluded.
- Psychology: exclusion aversion / FOMO. The viewer stays to get let in.
- Patterns: "There's a sneaky thing all the big [niche] players are doing and
  nobody is noticing." / "Why is nobody talking about this?" / "Only the
  smartest [audience] know about this tactic."
- Maps to families: curiosity gap, authority.

## 3. Lab Rat (live experiment)
Run an experiment on your own account or life, usually in real time.
- Psychology: tangible outcome + visible progress/transformation = sustained
  curiosity. "I tried it so you don't have to."
- Patterns: "Can I do [hard thing] with only [constraint]? Let's find out." /
  "I swapped [my normal thing] for [what the gurus do] and here's what
  happened." / "I tested [claim] for 30 days."
- Maps to families: interactive/challenge, transformation, result reveal.

## 4. Expert (credibility first)
Lead with a reason to listen: your own expertise or borrowed authority.
- Psychology: authority bias. Credibility up front buys the next 10 seconds.
- Patterns: "As a [profession/identity], here's the number one [X] I see."
  Works with ANY identity, not just credentials ("as a bus driver", "as a
  stay-at-home mum"). Borrowed version: "I read 30 books about [topic], here
  are the 3 most surprising facts."
- Maps to families: authority/earned flex, time-credibility.

## 5. Mirror (reflect the audience)
Reflect the viewer's own situation straight back at them.
- Psychology: self-recognition. "That's me" is the strongest stop there is,
  and it drives shares ("someone finally said it").
- Patterns: "Only [specific identity] will understand this." / "If you're
  struggling with [specific pain], you need to hear this."
- Maps to family: targeted call-out. Usually the default trigger for a
  relatable secondary lane.

## 6. Sledgehammer (bold contrarian strike)
Say something strong, bold and controversial that goes against what most
people believe.
- Psychology: maximum contrast. Cognitive dissonance forces a reaction, and
  the comment section does the distribution (pairs with the engineered-debate
  principle).
- Patterns: "I think [group] are doing [accepted good thing] too much." /
  "[Sacred practice] is a waste of time." Must be defensible, not rage-bait.
- Maps to family: contrarian/myth-bust.

## Rules of use (the part that matters)

1. **Classify every hook.** For each script's `text_hook`/`spoken_hook`, know
   which of the six it fires. None = topic = rewrite (same law as "the hook is
   a true tension, never a topic").
2. **Stack two where natural.** Example stack: Expert ("I studied thousands of
   videos") + Insider ("six styles are blowing up right now") + Crystal Ball
   ("you'll start seeing them everywhere"). Stacked hooks outperform single
   ones; do not force a third.
3. **Spread across the batch.** Do not let one style dominate a run. Rough
   fit by intent: educational leans Expert/Insider, storytelling leans Lab
   Rat/Expert, hot-take leans Sledgehammer/Crystal Ball, relatable lane leans
   Mirror.
4. **Curiosity + contrast check.** Whatever the style, the line must open a
   gap (curiosity) and break a pattern or expectation (contrast). A hook with
   curiosity but no contrast is mild; contrast but no curiosity is just a
   shout.
5. **Record it.** Each script item may carry a `hook_styles` array (e.g.
   `["lab-rat", "insider"]`); the dashboard renders them as badges.
