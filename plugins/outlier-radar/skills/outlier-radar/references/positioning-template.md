# Positioning template (fill from radar-config.json)

This is the fill-in-the-blanks positioning brief the engine reads before writing
any script. Everything in `{{curly braces}}` is pulled from `radar-config.json`,
written during first-run discovery. Rewrite the prose in your own words once the
placeholders are filled; treat this as the skeleton, not the final copy.

`{{creator}}` is anchoring their personal brand on **{{niche}}**: the one-line
subject they want to be known for. Not a generic guru version of it. This
specific angle. Everything the engine writes serves that anchor.

## The lens every primary-lane (Industry) script must pass through
- It is about the CORE IDEA of `{{niche}}`, not tactics-for-tactics-sake.
- Favourite tensions: rotate the `{{facets}}` (the 2 to 4 sub-angles in the
  config), plus whatever real contrarian truths the creator holds in this niche.
  Each script should land on one clear tension, not a soup of them.
- Default villain (optional): the common belief or lazy habit in `{{niche}}` that
  `{{creator}}` thinks is wrong. Name it if the config gives one; skip if not.

## Two content lanes only
1. **{{primary_lane.label}}** (default "Industry", primary): teach real, concrete
   value in `{{niche}}`. Educational and insight-led. Marry a proven viral
   mechanic to genuine substance from the creator's own expertise (see
   `{{methods_file}}` for the prescription source).
2. **{{secondary_lane.label}}** (default "Viral videos", secondary, optional):
   reach plays adjacent to the niche. Borrow the hook and format from a real
   viral creator in an adjacent lane and pour in the creator's actual world.
   Lighter and less educational than the Industry lane, but still true to who
   `{{creator}}` is so it compounds authority rather than diluting it. Pure
   relatable or entertaining content is fine here; no forced selling.

## Voice
Use `{{voice}}` from the config verbatim as the guide. In the absence of detail:
premise-first, blunt, compressed, contrarian-but-grounded, real numbers for
credibility. No em dashes or en dashes anywhere in output.

## What the creator sells and the funnel phase (read before writing LinkedIn)
`{{creator}}` positions as an expert in `{{niche}}`. The commercial ladder,
in order:
1. **Authority (primary, now)** build the reputation as THE person for `{{niche}}`.
2. **Short-term** whatever leads or reach support the creator's current goal
   (fill from config or discovery).
3. **Later** the eventual offer (consulting, product, advisory, whatever applies).

Default assumption: the creator is in the **authority plus audience-growth phase**.
Content must DEMONSTRATE hire-able expertise (frameworks, diagnoses, how the thing
actually works), but the CTA stays **top-of-funnel: follow and grow**. Do not
pitch, do not push DMs or calls, do not plug the eventual offer in posts yet. The
expertise builds the authority; the authority does the selling later. Adjust only
if the config or the creator says they are already in a selling phase.

## Two channels, two different jobs (this matters)
- **Short-form video (TikTok/Reels/Shorts) = the REACH lane.** Value can be
  entertainment, shock, or relatability, because the job is attention and
  audience growth. Skits and bits are fine here.
- **LinkedIn = the AUTHORITY / SELL lane.** Lean EDUCATIONAL and EXPERTISE-forward.
  Most posts should teach something real about `{{niche}}` (a framework, a
  diagnosis, "here is how it actually works") so a buyer concludes the creator is
  the expert. Keep roughly one pure contrarian post per batch for reach, but the
  dominant mode is demonstrate-expertise plus buyer-pain. Use the linkedin-engine
  skill if `{{linkedin_twins}}` is true, never a throwaway one-liner. CTAs stay
  top-of-funnel while in the authority phase: a follow, a sharp question, or a
  "what's your take" prompt. No pitching, no DM or call asks, no plugging the
  offer yet.
