## How to turn a method into a script's do-this beat
A good educational script names the shift (the reframe), then hands ONE concrete,
repeatable move the viewer can do this week, drawn from the list above. Generic
worked example, using a placeholder method "read the real signal before acting":

- Reframe: "Most people guess what their audience wants. You do not have to guess."
- Do-this beat: "This week, go to the one place your people actually talk, pull
  the five questions they keep asking, and make your next piece answer one of
  them directly."
- Honest timeline: "You will not see it move in a day. Give it a few weeks of
  doing this every time, and the pattern compounds."

That is the full arc: name the shift, hand one move, anchor it to an honest
timeline. Always pair the move with the realistic timeline so it stays honest.
