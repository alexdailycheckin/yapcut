# Leaders to study (roster template)

**Optional section.** This whole LinkedIn/creator-scan is gated by `linkedin_twins` in `radar-config.json` (and by whether you actually publish on the channels these leaders use). If you do not run a LinkedIn or written-post lane, skip this file entirely. If you do, this is a roster of the leaders and creators in YOUR niche whose content is currently working, that the weekly run studies for hook + structure + why-it-worked, then turns the sharpest mechanics into your own scripts.

North star: study the best operators in your space, not the loudest posters. Pick people whose work you would want to be mistaken for.

**Hard exclusion:** decide up front what is out of scope for your niche and drop it (for example, if you teach organic content, exclude anyone whose posts are mainly paid-ads mechanics). If a single post from a roster member is off-scope even though the person usually fits, skip that post, not the person.

We do not log a post just because a big name wrote it. We log posts that **overperformed** (clearly above that person's own baseline: high comments-to-followers, heavy reshares, or written up / re-shared elsewhere) AND carry a transferable lesson. Same logic as the video radar: the signal is that a hook and structure worked.

## Scan method (web-research flow, no login)

LinkedIn and most feeds are auth-walled, so we cannot crawl them directly. Instead, per run:
1. For ~6-10 roster members (rotate so everyone gets covered over a few weeks; always include the one or two whose voice you most want to model), web-search for their recent standout posts: `"<name>" linkedin post` (or the relevant platform) plus terms like `viral`, `breakdown`, `most comments`, last ~4-8 weeks. Public post URLs can be fetched with WebFetch; aggregator/teardown articles fill the rest.
2. Keep only posts that overperformed AND teach something. Aim for 5-8 logged finds per week.
3. For each, capture: **author**, **link**, **hook** (verbatim first line), **structure** (the 2-4 beat skeleton), **why it worked** (the one transferable mechanic), **format** (text / carousel / video / image), and **what you can steal** (one line).
4. Always include a citation (the post URL or the article that surfaced it). Flag when engagement is estimated.

## Outputs

1. **Radar log:** append the week's finds to your leaders radar log (see `leaders_file` in `radar-config.json`), newest on top. Then turn the 2-3 sharpest mechanics into scripts in the normal Outlier Radar run (premise-first, in your voice), so the learning becomes content, not just notes.
2. **Anywhere else you keep durable patterns:** append reusable patterns (not one-offs) to your own library so they compound over time.
